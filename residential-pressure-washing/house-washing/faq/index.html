House Washing

House Washing
Questions Answered

Soft wash, cladding compatibility, frequency, and what to expect on the day. Real answers from the team that does the work.

We almost always soft wash siding. High pressure can damage cedar shakes, stucco, vinyl seams, and painted surfaces. Soft washing combines low pressure with biodegradable cleaning solutions that lift algae, mildew, and grime safely. The result lasts longer because the solution kills growth at the source rather than just rinsing it off.

All common residential claddings can be cleaned safely with the right method: vinyl, cedar shakes and shingles, Hardie board, stucco, brick, painted wood, aluminum, and engineered composites. We assess the cladding before choosing pressure and solution, and we test a small area first when surfaces are unfamiliar.

Every 12 to 24 months is right for most coastal BC homes. North Shore properties, homes with mature tree cover, and waterfront properties often benefit from annual cycles. Salt air, heavy rainfall, and shade combine to grow algae quickly here.

Not when applied correctly. We rinse plantings before and after, dilute solutions appropriately, and tarp delicate beds when needed. Our biodegradable mixtures break down naturally and are designed for use around residential landscaping. We treat the surrounding garden as carefully as the wall we are cleaning.

Properly applied soft wash will not remove sound paint. If paint is already failing, peeling, or chalking heavily, the wash may reveal that condition rather than cause it. We point this out during the assessment so there are no surprises.

Most single-family homes take three to six hours, depending on size, height, and the level of staining. Larger estate homes and multi-storey properties may take a full day. We give you a time estimate during the assessment.

Yes. Soft wash equipment reaches three storeys and beyond from the ground in most configurations, which is both safer and gentler than ladder-based work. Hillside and architecturally complex homes are part of regular work, especially on the North Shore and West Vancouver.

Not necessarily. Many clients are away during the work, especially for recurring programmes. We just need access to an exterior water source and clear paths around the home. We will confirm scope and any specific requests before we start.

Heavy rain and freezing temperatures stop work. Light rain is fine. We work through most of the year in Greater Vancouver. If we have to reschedule for weather we will give you as much notice as possible.

Eventually, yes. Algae and mildew are part of living in a coastal climate. The biodegradable solutions we use kill growth at the source, which delays return significantly compared with simple rinse-off cleaning. Most properties stay visibly clean for 12 to 24 months after a thorough soft wash.
