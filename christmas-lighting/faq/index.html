Holiday Lighting
Questions Answered

Booking timeline, custom design, light types, removal, storage, and what it really costs. Real answers from the team that does the work.

October books first for the most desirable installation dates, and the calendar fills quickly. We accept bookings as early as September. Custom design and large displays benefit from the longest lead time, so call early if your property is unique or you want a specific look.

We supply professional-grade commercial lights and configure them to your property's roofline, landscape, and trees. Using our lights ensures the right gauge wiring, proper bulb spacing, weather sealing, and a clean look year over year. We also stock multiple bulb styles and colour temperatures so the design fits your home.

We use commercial-grade LED in C9, C7, mini light, and warm-white or pure-white configurations. Colour options include warm white, cool white, multicolour, and seasonal palettes. We also work with wreaths, garland, tree wraps, ground stakes, and pathway lighting.

Yes. The on-site assessment is also a design conversation. We walk the property, talk through the architectural lines and the look you want, and propose a configuration. Custom design is the difference between adequate and exceptional, especially on larger or architecturally complex homes.

Pricing depends on roofline length, tree and landscape features, light type and quality, design complexity, and removal logistics. Most residential installations in Greater Vancouver fall in a meaningful range. Once we have walked the property we provide a written estimate. Assessments are complimentary.

Yes. We configure power runs, splits, and timers to keep load balanced and operation reliable. Most installations run on standard exterior outlets with weather-rated timers. For larger displays we may recommend additional outlets, which we will flag during the design conversation.

Mid-season service is part of every install. If a string goes out we come back and fix it. The point of professional installation is that you do not have to spend a December weekend on a ladder diagnosing wiring problems.

Removal happens in January, weather permitting. We coordinate dates with you and bring everything down carefully. Lights are inspected, sorted, and stored at our facility for next season, which means no tangled strings in your garage and no storage space to find for spare bulbs and timers.

Yes. Storage is included. Your specific configuration is labelled and held at our facility, ready for reinstall the following autumn. This makes year-over-year continuity straightforward and means lights tend to last longer because they are stored properly.

Absolutely. Many clients refine their display each year as they see what works on their property. Adding tree wrap, expanding to a second roofline, or shifting colour palette is straightforward. We schedule the conversation in early autumn before that year's install.

Yes. Storefronts, office buildings, retail centres, hotels, and strata complexes are all part of our holiday work. Commercial installations are scheduled around operating hours and tenant access. Recurring multi-year programmes are common in this category.

Greater Vancouver weather usually allows install and removal as planned. When conditions are unsafe we reschedule rather than risk damage to the property or our crew. We give you as much notice as possible if a date needs to shift.
