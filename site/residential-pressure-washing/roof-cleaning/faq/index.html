Roof Cleaning

Roof Cleaning
Questions Answered

Soft wash, gloeocapsa, moss, manufacturer warranties, and what we will not do to your shingles. Real answers from the team that does the work.

No, when soft-washed properly. We never use high-pressure water on shingles. Pressure washing dislodges granules and shortens roof life. Soft washing applies low-pressure biodegradable solutions that kill moss, lichen, algae, and gloeocapsa magma without harming the shingle surface.

Gloeocapsa magma is a cyanobacterium that appears as black streaks on asphalt shingles, especially on north-facing slopes. It feeds on the limestone in shingle granules and accelerates roof deterioration. Left untreated, it shortens roof life by years. Routine cleaning removes it and protects the lifespan of the roof.

Black streaks running down a roof are usually gloeocapsa magma. Moss appears as raised, green or grey-green clumps that hold moisture against shingles. Lichen looks like grey or pale-green crusty patches that bond to the surface. We treat all three with appropriate biodegradable solutions, with method tuned to severity.

Only when necessary and only on roof types and pitches that allow it safely. Most soft-wash roof cleaning is performed from ladders, platforms, or extension equipment that lets us work without putting weight on the shingles. Avoiding foot traffic is part of how we extend roof life.

Most Greater Vancouver roofs benefit from cleaning every two to four years. Properties under heavy tree cover, on the North Shore, or in areas of high humidity may need more frequent attention. We can recommend a cycle that fits your specific exposure during the assessment.

A properly applied soft wash continues working after we leave, killing residual organisms and delaying regrowth. Most properties stay visibly clean for two to four years. Adding zinc or copper strips to the ridge can extend results further by inhibiting growth between cleanings.

Soft wash cleaning conforms to most major shingle manufacturer maintenance recommendations. Pressure washing typically does not. Because we soft wash exclusively, our work supports rather than threatens manufacturer warranty terms. We are happy to share our process documentation if your warranty requires it.

Yes. Soft wash methods are effective on asphalt, metal, concrete tile, clay tile, and flat or low-slope commercial roofing. Each surface has different cleaning solution and pressure considerations, which we adjust accordingly. Tile roofs especially benefit from professional handling because they are slippery and easy to crack.
