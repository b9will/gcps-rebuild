Pressure & Soft Wash Residential

Pressure washing uses high-pressure water to clean hard surfaces like concrete and brick. Soft washing uses low pressure combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions to safely clean delicate surfaces such as siding, roofing, and stucco. The right method depends entirely on the surface, and we choose based on what protects your property best.

Not when the right method is used. We almost always soft-wash siding with low pressure and a biodegradable solution rather than blasting it. High-pressure water can damage cedar shakes, stucco, vinyl seams, and paint. Our crews assess the cladding type before choosing technique.

For most coastal BC homes, every 12 to 24 months keeps siding free of algae and mildew. North Shore properties and homes with mature tree cover often benefit from annual cycles. Houses near the ocean may need more frequent attention because of salt air.

Yes, when soft-washed properly. We never use high pressure on shingles. Our biodegradable solutions kill moss, lichen, algae, and gloeocapsa magma at the source without dislodging granules or shortening shingle life. The treatment also continues working after we leave, preventing regrowth for longer.

Gloeocapsa magma is a cyanobacterium that appears as black streaks on asphalt shingles. It feeds on the limestone in the shingle granules and accelerates roof deterioration. Routine cleaning removes it and protects the lifespan of your roof. More on roof cleaning →

Window Cleaning Methods & Cadence

For most coastal BC homes, twice a year is the right cadence. Spring removes pollen and winter grime, autumn handles the accumulated dust and salt air from summer. Homes near the ocean, near construction, or under heavy tree cover may want to add a wipe between cycles.

Water-fed poles excel on exterior glass on multi-storey homes, sunrooms, and skylights, with the safety advantage of staying on the ground. Traditional squeegees are unmatched for interior glass and ground-floor exterior work where a hand finish reads cleaner. Most quality cleaners use both on the same property.

Yes. Interior cleaning is most often done with a traditional squeegee, microfibre cloth, and detail work on tracks and sills. We coordinate timing with you to minimise disruption inside the home.

Concrete & Pavers Sealing & Sand

Sealing creates a protective barrier against UV exposure, moisture, staining, and organic growth. Sealed surfaces resist the wear that coastal weather inflicts and are significantly easier to maintain. Sealing also preserves the colour and texture of stamped concrete and pavers.

Most concrete and paver surfaces need re-sealing every two to four years, depending on exposure, traffic, and the type of sealer applied. Penetrating sealers tend to last longer than topical film-formers. We assess your surface and recommend the right cycle.

Yes. Polymeric jointing sand stabilises pavers, deters weeds, and prevents erosion. We re-sand after cleaning when joints have washed out, which is common after a few seasons of rain.
